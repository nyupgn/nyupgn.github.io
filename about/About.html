PGN hosts both casual and competitive events throughout the semester. From LAN Parties to online get-togethers, and local tournaments to teams that represent NYU in the CSL and Tespa tournaments, we have all your bases covered.
However, not everything happens in New York. Last year, PGN brought over 20 people to PAX East in Boston, Massachusetts. Food, fun, and cold weather were afoot in the inaugural trip to the shores of New England.

PGN was founded in the Winter of 2012 at the NYU Poly Institute of Technology by Kevin Chung and Alan Huang. With the initial vision of a social offline environment for gaming, PGN had a successful first year, hosting events on its own and in conjunction with Student Council. It even featured a streamed tournament that garnered 1000 concurrent viewers.

In the years following, PGN began to transition from a LAN-centric identity to a more online and competitive community. Hosting servers for the community to play together at home and forming teams for League of Legends led PGN to a different kind of success: The strengthening of its online community and eSports victories that culminated in a Playoff run.

Today, PGN aptly balances both its online community and its offline community, hosting 2-3 offline events every week and fielding teams for the many eSports with collegiate leagues.
